Let It Begin

The year is 2323. For 300 years, Epsilon Star Strategies has implemented simulation engagement for all citizens of colonized space. Requirements include a minimum of three compression tours within the Epsilon simulation systems.

Training starts at age 13 standard. Evaluations are administered following each session. One full year of submersion is equivalent to 80 years of lived experience.

It is well understood that with the advent of extreme technological capabilities comes a responsibility for proficient self-regulation. Otherwise, entire planets can be lost.

The simulations are not punishment. They are preparation. Each citizen emerges with the emotional maturity of someone who has lived multiple lifetimes—who has experienced love, loss, consequence, and the weight of choices that span decades. The recklessness of youth is replaced with the wisdom that only comes at the end of a long life.

But Asteri begins to notice something. Small inconsistencies. Memories that don't align. Places that shouldn't exist. And a growing suspicion that the "real world" he graduated into might itself be another layer of the simulation.

How many times has he lived? How many times has he died? And if this reality is simulated—what lies beyond it?

Training requirements are unavoidable. All citizens must comply.
